There are a few things to know before before you dive into understanding beer production. Before you dive into beer production there is something important to know.
Brewers don't make beer yeast does! We'll get back to that in a minute. There are two main parts to beer production.

The first part of beer production is actually brewing. This is otherwise known as the hot side of production. During this process the brewers make a sugary liquid wort.

  1. Malted barley and grains are steeped in hot water
  2. The grains are rinsed with hot water and the liquid separated into a boil kettle
  3. The liquid called wort is boiled with hops, a bittering agent
  4. The bitter wort is cooled town and moved over to a fermenter

The second stage begins as soon as the wort is chilled and moved to the fermenter. Anything done at this point on is known as the cold side of production.

  1. Yeast is added to the chilled wort and now can be referred to as beer
  2. The yeast eats the sugars and converts it into alcohol
  3. When the yeast is done fermenting the beer is chilled and the contents allowed to settle
  4. The beer is then separated from the settled contents carbonated and packaged
