How to consume craft beer

A guide to being a great craft beer consumer

How to Love Beer How Beer Is Made Craft Beer Styles How to Consume

The most important part of craft beer is how to properly consume it! By consuming I don't mean this is a guide to tell you how to actually drink your beer.
This is a guide to how to find the best possible product and ways to enjoy it.

Purchasing package beer from a store can be a bit intimidating if there is a large selection, or you don't know what you like.
Purchasing beer that is out of date or poorly handled can leave a bad taste in your mouth.
Sometimes this is completely out of the breweries hand, and can keep you exploring beers from great breweries.

Here are a few pointers for making sure you get the best quality package.

  • First and most important is to check the date! Don't buy old or aging product
  • Look for styles you enjoy or have learned about and want to try. Check the BJCP Style Guidelines for suggestions
  • Try to shop at a store that stores the beer cold at all times. Most craft beer is unfiltered and unpasteurized.

Another great way to enjoy craft beer is with food. With so many beer styles out available there are just as many foods you can pair with every one.
My favorite way to enjoy craft beer with food is meals cooked in my own kitchen. That being said not everyone enjoys cooking at home the way i do.
Luckily, the're are quite a few ways to get great food beer parings.

  • Brewpubs are a great because its a brewery and restaurant. Fresh local beer with food made to pair
  • Your local brewery is also another great way to get some great pairings. Most breweries have different food trucks throughout the week
  • Local restaurants can be some of the best dining experiences.
    With a lot of restaurants focusing on local ingredients you can be sure to find great local beers

The last way to be a good Craft Beer consumer is to be a positive part of the craft beer community.
It's the most important and can help you really get the great feel of the craft beer community.

  • Be respectful to the place and the people. Think golden rule meets the hikers motto.
  • Support local! Craft beer employs a lot of people and families. Also, fresh local beer is by far the best
  • Just enjoy yourself. Craft beer doesn't have to be complicated, and is best enjoyed with the people you care for the most
